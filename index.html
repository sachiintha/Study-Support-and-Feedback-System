Research Project Scope

Study Support and Feedback System Using Natural Language Processing (NLP) aims to provide an abstraction layer for a system that assists in studying and providing feedback to users through the application of NLP techniques. The system employs NLP algorithms and methodologies to analyze and understand user input in natural language, paving way for personalized study support and feedback. The abstraction encompasses receiving natural language input from users, which can include structured and essay questions targeting feedback on study materials. NLP techniques are applied to preprocess, tokenize, and parse the input, extracting relevant keyword information and determining the user's intent. NLP algorithms focus on providing a highly accurate score with constructive feedback which enlighten the students to reflect on their progress with time. The system has a crucial influence to generate tailored responses to user queries or feedback, enabling better learning. Coherent and informative natural language responses are provided in a clear and concise manner which eases the work of teachers and lecturers. Students gain the opportunity to seek on areas which need improvement, and teachers can aid them to have a remedy through the system. This helps to generate an efficient and wholesome study environment with a positive impact for both students and teachers. Users can productively interact with the study support and feedback system through a convenient and user-friendly interface. By abstracting the system into these components, the Study Support and Feedback System Using NLP targets to enhance the studying experience, provide personalized support, and deliver effective feedback to users through the application of NLP techniques.

Literature Survey

Natural Language Processing (NLP) has advanced massively in recent years, enabling the creation of intelligent computers that can comprehend and analyze input in natural language. As a result, support and feedback systems have been developed to aid students in their learning process by offering individualized help, feedback, and direction [7]. Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) have been the primary focus of conventional assistance and feedback systems, while Structured Essay Questions (SEQs) have become increasingly well-liked due to their capacity to evaluate higher-order thinking abilities. NLP algorithms can comprehend and grade these answers, giving both students and teachers valuable feedback. Key phrase extraction and ontology mapping are utilized to identify the most relevant words or ideas in a text and cater individualized feedback [8]. The development of a support and feedback system for SEQs that makes use of key phrase extraction, NLP, and ML algorithms, as well as ontology mapping, has the potential to entirely alter the manner instructors evaluate and give feedback on students' learning. NLP algorithms, key phrase extraction, and ontology mapping have the potential to revolutionize the field of education by upgrading the process of SEQ evaluation and feedback. This system is capable of analyzing and assessing student responses to SEQs with accuracy, and provide individualized and substantive feedback. It can help to overcome the drawbacks of conventional support and feedback systems, which often rely on MCQs [9]. Additionally, it can be used to monitor student development over time and spot areas where they might require more assistance or resources. This approach has the potential to uplift educational standards overall and improve student learning results drastically.

Methodolgy

The initial phase entails gathering a sizable and varied dataset of graded open-ended questions from prior online tests. In order to train machine learning algorithms, this dataset will be utilized. To ensure uniformity in the grading standards and to remove any redundant or extraneous information, the obtained dataset will then be preprocessed. The preprocessed dataset would then be used to extract the pertinent features. These features, which can be utilized to train machine learning algorithms, may comprise linguistic, semantic, and syntactic elements. Selection of the most appropriate machine learning algorithm for grading open-ended questions in online exams will include evaluating and comparing several different methods. Using the retrieved features from the preprocessed dataset, the chosen machine learning method would be trained. By contrasting the developed machine learning model's grading accuracy with that of human graders, its performance would be assessed. To make sure that the model can generalize to new queries, evaluation would be conducted on a separate distinct test dataset. The resulting machine learning model would next be implemented into an online examination system to automatically grade open-ended questions. The proposed online examination question correcting system would be put to the test in a real-world online examination environment to assure its usefulness.
